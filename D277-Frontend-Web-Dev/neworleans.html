New Orleans

About New Orleans

New Orleans, Louisiana's most famous city, is a destination like no other. Known for its lively atmosphere, rich history, and diverse culture, the city sits near the mouth of the Mississippi River and has long been a crossroads of French, Spanish, African, and Creole influences. Visitors can wander through the historic French Quarter, where narrow streets are lined with colorful buildings, wrought-iron balconies, jazz clubs, and vibrant marketplaces. The city is synonymous with music, especially jazz, and local clubs and festivals provide countless opportunities to enjoy live performances throughout the year.

The Garden District offers a quieter contrast to the bustling French Quarter. Its tree-lined streets, stately mansions, and charming boutiques display the elegance of the city's antebellum past. Festivals are central to the New Orleans experience. Mardi Gras, the city's most iconic celebration, draws visitors from around the world with its colorful parades, masks, costumes, and street parties. The New Orleans Jazz & Heritage Festival is another highlight, showcasing world-class music, local culinary specialties, and arts from across Louisiana.

New Orleans is also a culinary paradise. Creole and Cajun dishes like gumbo, jambalaya, po'boys, red beans and rice, and beignets define the city's rich food culture. The city has a fascinating history that can be explored through historic plantations, museums, and landmarks that tell the story of its diverse past. Outdoor activities include riverboat cruises along the Mississippi and guided swamp tours where visitors can spot native wildlife such as alligators, herons, and egrets. With its unforgettable music, food, and cultural experiences, New Orleans offers visitors an immersive journey into Louisiana's soul.

Top 3 Things to Do

  1. Pop into the French Quarter for attractions such as Jackson Square, St. Louis Cathedral, and the infamous Bourbon Street
  2. Marvel at elegant 19th-century architecture and dreamy trees that form a canopy along the streets of the Garden District
  3. Join the party at Mardi Gras, Jazz Fest, or any of the dozens of festivals hosted throughout the city
